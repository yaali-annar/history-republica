# The 20th Century

## The Long Peace

The name came later. In 1905, nobody called it that — the Final War had just ended, the Covenant was dissolving, and the immediate work was reconstruction: reconstituting broken states, negotiating the post-Covenant settlement, absorbing Alaska into the Northwest Territory, watching the UKSA fragment into its constituent republics one by one. The name the century eventually gave to itself was not available at its beginning.

By 2000, it is simply what the century is called. The Long Peace. One hundred years without a major war between recognised states. The League of Nations, which the cynics of 1905 expected to follow the Maritime League into ceremonial irrelevance, turned out to be the instrument that made it hold.
