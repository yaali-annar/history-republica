# Faction Document Template

This file is a development reference. Copy the structure below when creating a new faction document.

---

## Header fields

Required fields appear in every document. Optional fields appear only where applicable.

```
# [Full common name of the faction]

**Official name**: [Full formal name, with local-language name in italics if applicable]
**Capital**: [City name (local name in italics if different)]
**Founded**: [Year or approximate date]
**Head of Government**: [Title and selection method]
**Head of State**: [If separate from Head of Government]
**League membership**: [Maritime League / League of Nations / associate / none, with date]

--- (optional fields below) ---

**Common name**: [Informal name used by outsiders]
**Constituent states**: [If a federation]
**Official languages**: [If multilingual and relevant]
**Government**: [If a brief label helps — "Federal republic", "Constitutional monarchy", etc.]
```

## Section reference

Not all sections appear in every document. Use what the faction needs. Order the narrative sections to suit the faction's story — there is no fixed sequence beyond Overview first and Position in the League last.

---

### ## Overview

Always present. Two to four paragraphs. Covers: what the faction is, where it sits in the world order, one or two defining characteristics. Should be readable as a standalone summary. Does not recapitulate every section that follows — leaves detail to the body.

---

### ## [Historical narrative sections]

The bulk of every document. These are named to fit the faction's specific history rather than using generic labels. Examples from existing documents:

- `## Colonial Origins`
- `## Road to Independence`
- `## The Formation of the Northern Realm`
- `## The Revolution of 1789`
- `## The Seven Years War and Treaty Day`
- `## Johan de Witt and the Long Pensionary Period`

Name sections after events or periods, not after generic categories like "History." Each section should carry a clear argument or through-line, not merely recount events.

---

### ## Constitutional Structure

Present in most documents. Covers the mechanics of governance: executive office, legislature, franchise, how authority is divided. Use `###` subsections for each element (franchise, executive title, parliament, etc.).

Useful subsections:
- `### [Executive title]` — selection method, term, powers, limits
- `### [Legislature]` — composition, authority, relationship to executive
- `### The Franchise` — who votes, how that has changed over time

---

### ## Internal Factions

Optional. Present where the faction's internal politics are legible and relevant. Describes major blocs, tendencies, or parties — not a comprehensive political survey, but the fault lines that matter for understanding how the faction behaves. Use `###` per faction.

---

### ## Society

Optional. Present where the population's composition, origins, or social structure is central to understanding the faction. Covers ethnic, religious, linguistic, or class groupings as relevant. Usually appears in documents where the population is diverse in origin or where social structure shaped the constitutional settlement.

---

### ## Strategic Orientation / Strategic Role

Optional. Present where the faction's external posture is a defining characteristic rather than simply a consequence of position. The Dutch Republic document uses this to explain the VOC's trading-post model vs. territorial conquest. The Cape Republic document uses it to cover the waystation function and Simon's Bay.

---

### ## Position in the League

Always present, usually last. Covers: founding member or later accession, what the faction contributes to and receives from the League, its diplomatic weight and relationships with other members. Should also note any complications — associate status, delayed admission, conditions attached to membership.

---

### ## TODO

Optional. Present where canon is incomplete. List open questions or pending development as bullet points. Remove when resolved.

---

## Style conventions

**Voice**: Third-person encyclopedic. Written as though by a knowledgeable observer in the Straits Federal District in the year 2000. Strictly in-world — no references to other timelines, counterfactuals, or divergence from anything else. Things happened as they happened.

**Prose**: Narrative paragraphs, not bullet lists. Reserve bullets for the TODO section and for geographic enumerations (e.g. listing borders). Favour long, structured paragraphs with clear topic sentences over fragmented lists.

**Non-English terms**: Italics on first use, Roman thereafter if used repeatedly. Include the original-language name for official titles and proper nouns where it adds texture.

**Key terms**: Bold for the first appearance of significant offices, documents, treaties, or institutional names within a section. Not bold for every recurrence.

**Subsections**: Use `###` for subsections within a `##` section. Use `####` sparingly, only if a `###` section itself needs subdivision.

**Dates**: Specific years where known. Approximate dates with "circa" or "the [decade]s" where uncertain. Avoid false precision.

**Length**: No fixed length. Documents should be as long as the faction requires and no longer. Existing documents range from roughly 1,500 to 4,000 words.
