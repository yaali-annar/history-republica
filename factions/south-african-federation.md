## Overview

The South African Federation is the independent state that emerged from the former Iberian Pink Strip following the 1905 Covenant collapse. It occupies a broad band of territory running east-west across the southern African continent, inheriting access to both the Atlantic coast (the former Angolan territories) and the Indian Ocean coast (the former Mozambican territories), with a vast interior corridor connecting them.

The territory was built as a geopolitical instrument — a land bridge designed to connect Iberian Atlantic and Indian Ocean possessions and contain League republicanism at the Cape — rather than as a coherent country. Its infrastructure serves that colonial logic: coast-to-coast routes for Iberian strategic movement, extraction networks for Covenant-era resources. Internal connectivity, civic institutions, and an economy oriented toward its own population are a post-independence development still incomplete in 2000.

The Federation is poor and struggling. It has coastlines and does not prosper from them.

## Population

The Federation's population is layered by the successive waves of Covenant investment.

**Indigenous majority**: Shona, Ndebele, Tonga, Lozi, Chewa, and other peoples across the interior and coastal regions. The majority of the population by a substantial margin.

**Iberian settler community**: Portuguese and Spanish descendants of the original colonial administrators and settlers. Catholic, Romance-speaking, concentrated in the coastal cities and administrative centres established during the Iberian period.

**Germanic settler community**: Prussian and Austrian descendants who arrived under the Colonial Protocol — the arrangement by which Prussia and Austria committed settlers, soldiers, and capital to Iberian colonial territories in exchange for a share of revenues. Lutheran and Catholic, German-speaking, they came as investors, engineers, and soldiers rather than farmers, and were concentrated in the interior corridor where the Protocol's infrastructure projects were based. After 1905, with the Covenant dissolved and their patron states transformed, this community lost its institutional backing and has had to find its place within an independent state that was never designed with them in mind.
